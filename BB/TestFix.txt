Corrections / clarifications

“Use -n 2 to avoid Qt resource exhaustion.”
Parallelism can hide leaks as much as it helps. Keep it for speed, but explicitly advise running the suite serially in CI or as a pre-merge job to catch accumulation/leak issues (e.g., nightly pytest -n 0 --maxfail=1). Reframe “exhaustion → use -n” to “exhaustion → fix leaks; -n for speed”.

processEvents() guidance.
Saying it “causes race conditions” is too absolute. The pattern that’s racy is pumping events without a condition. Keep your replacement helpers, but clarify that processEvents() is fine when wrapped in a condition-based wait (which your wait_for_condition already exemplifies).

You advise “don’t manually clear caches” (thanks to an autouse fixture) yet also show manual cache_manager.clear_cache() in examples. Tighten this rule:

Default: rely on the autouse cache clearer.

Exception: if a test mutates persistent cache on disk as part of behaviour under test, then clean it explicitly in that test.

Duck-typing tests.
The hasattr example is fine for capability probes, but advise readers to prefer testing the call site behaviour over asserting “object has method X”. Otherwise you risk tests that pass while behaviour still breaks.
